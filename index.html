Singapore Polytechnic

This is Singapore Polytechnic (SP), a place where the majority of students discover their career paths and opportunities, achieve their goals, plan for workshops or university, and enjoy events and co-curricular activities. Also, a place where new friendships are formed, students from different nationalities interact, and skills are honed.

Welcome to the SP70 Commemoration Page, where we honor 70 years of passion, innovation, and community. As we celebrate this milestone, we invite you to join us in reflecting on our rich history and looking forward to an even brighter future.

Time flies! In 2024, Singapore Polytechnic has turned 70. Many changes have taken place since the campus was first established on October 7, 1954. SP is the first polytechnic in Singapore. According to its history, it began in a humble five-storey building on Prince Edward Road and grew into Singapore's largest polytechnic, with a campus spanning 38 hectares of lush greenery.

The Evolution of SP

In the seventies, SP was expanded to three campuses due to the demands: one at Prince Edward Road, another at Dover called Princess Mary campus, and another at Ayer Rajah Road. In 1979, Princess Mary campus was replaced by new campus, which become the permanent campus of SP.

SP Crest

In 1960, Dr Toh Chin Chye (Deputy Prime Minister of Singapore and Chairman of the Board of Governors' of Singapore Polytechnic) suggested the Malay phrase "Berkhidmat Dengan Keahlian" translating to "To serve with skill" during a Board of Governors meeting. In 2014, this phrase evolved to "To serve the mastery," while retaining the Malay term "keahlian" which also signifies "mastery".

Additionally, Dr Toh has recommended two colour options for the SP crest: blue and pale violent or red and yellow. On May 17, 1960, SP's Board of Governors adopted the crest in red and yellow attached with significant deep meaning.
